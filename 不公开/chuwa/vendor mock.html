自我介绍

My name is Ethan Yu, and I am a Java full stack developer with a strong passion for enhancing and building web applications. I have had the opportunity to work for several companies, most recently at AT&T. My journey in the tech industry has been marked by a commitment to developing cross-platform applications using core Java and a wide array of frameworks such as Spring and microservice architecture.

Regarding my educational background, I earned my Master’s degree in Computer Science from the University of Southern California (USC) and obtained a Bachelor’s degree from American University in Washington, D.C.

project

project介绍

So, in my project, I focused on upgrading the inventory management system for AT&T’s online store. You know, they sell all sorts of products like phones, accessories, and service plans. The main goal was to create a system that could automatically track inventory in real-time. This means we can keep the stock levels just right, avoid running out of items, and make sure everything runs smoothly. The main idea was to make shopping better for customers by making sure they don’t face any issues with out-of-stock items and to help the store run more efficiently.

What features have you contributed?

I focused on setting up Kafka to handle real-time inventory updates, making sure we always knew what was in stock. I also used Spring to get all the parts talking to each other smoothly, so the inventory info was always up to date and accurate. Plus, I worked on fixing any errors that popped up, making sure everything ran smoothly.

hardest part

the hardest part was integrating Kafka with our backend. We needed to make sure it could handle a lot of updates fast without slowing things down. You know, keeping everything stable while doing all that was definitely a challenge. It really made us work hard to keep everything running smoothly and manage the real-time data well.

What kind of tech do you use?

We’re using some cool tech for this project. Mainly, we’ve got Kafka for handling all our real-time data streaming, which is key for keeping our inventory updates on point. Then, there’s Spring, which we use to tie everything together on the backend, making sure all our services communicate effectively. These tools help us maintain a smooth and efficient inventory system.

team size and team members and roles

Our team has 10 members: 3 in backend, 3 in frontend, 2 in QA, and 1 manager,

How does your team do the meeting?

Sure, we use Agile methods, so we have daily stand-ups to share updates and tackle any problems. We start each sprint with a planning meeting to decide on our goals, and at the end, we have a review meeting to talk about what we’ve done. We also have grooming sessions to prepare our tasks for the next sprint. For all these meetings, we use Zoom.
